はじまり

The first feeling

On the first day, we were nervous and did not really know what to feel. We had heard that the native Japanese teacher had gone, and that an Indian Japanese teacher would take the classes.

That was our first thought about Sivapriya Sensei. At first, we were not very connected with Sensei because we had only a minimum number of classes and meetings inside the campus.

つながり

The bond grew slowly

Then we started knowing more about Sensei and got to know each other. Still, most of our talks were only lesson based at that time.

Then came the summer classes, where we had to spend our whole day in Japanese classes with Sensei. That made a good bond between Sensei and the students. She was friendly, kind, and made learning practical.

感謝

Special thanks to Sivapriya Sensei

Sivapriya Sensei always supported the students through every kind of problem. She stood by us, helped us learn, and helped us improve in many things beyond Japanese.

She always wanted her students to stand out in a crowd of Japanese people. We will all miss Sensei and will be waiting for Sivapriya Sensei to return to CIT, because despite so many problems of her own, she always supported the students.
